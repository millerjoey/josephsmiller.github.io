What this is

Optimal experiment design is a decision system: at each step, pick the next sample that maximally reduces uncertainty about the outcome you care about, subject to budget and constraints.

Example: in deduplication, instead of labeling random record pairs, we label the pairs that most clarify cluster membership.

One adaptive engine that plugs into surveys, A/B tests, pricing experiments, and ML labeling.

Where it works

Practical sequential design that runs fast, even with large candidate spaces.

Best fit: when each measurement has real cost (time, money, risk) and you can choose what to measure next.

How it works

01
Define the decision and budget

Clarify the outcome, cost, and what it means to stop, so everyone agrees what "done" means.

02
Model uncertainty

Bayesian or approximate Bayesian models, so uncertainty is explicit, not guessed.

03
Choose the next data

Maximize expected information or utility under constraints, so each new measurement is worth the cost.

04
Stop early with confidence

Act when uncertainty is low enough to make the call, so you stop spending once the decision is clear.

Why this approach

Better experiments when data collection is expensive.

Most teams collect data uniformly because it is simple. The problem is that not all data points are equally valuable. Adaptive experiment and labeling systems quantify uncertainty and pick the next most informative measurement so you learn faster and stop earlier.

Fewer labels to reach the same accuracy. Faster decisions under budget. Clear uncertainty about whether more data is worth it.

Privacy note: when you run the demos locally, your files stay on your machine.

FAQ

Quick answers to common pilot questions.

Do we have to be fully Bayesian?

No. We can use approximate posteriors or uncertainty proxies when needed.

Does this work with my existing model?

Usually yes: we wrap your model with an acquisition rule and stopping criteria.

What data do you need for a pilot?

A candidate pool to sample from, a labeling/measurement mechanism, and a decision metric.

Can we run locally and keep data private?

Yes. Demos run locally; production deployments can be private.
