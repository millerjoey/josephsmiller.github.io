What this is

Optimal experiment design is a decision system: at each step, pick the next sample that maximally reduces uncertainty about the outcome you care about, subject to budget and constraints.

Example: in deduplication, instead of labeling random record pairs, we label the pairs that most clarify cluster membership.

One adaptive engine that plugs into surveys, A/B tests, pricing experiments, and ML labeling.

Method

A high-level view of sequential design.

Sequential design treats data collection as a control problem: choose the next measurement to maximize expected utility (or information) with respect to a target quantity of interest, while accounting for uncertainty and constraints.

In practice, this is implemented by maintaining an uncertainty-aware model, evaluating candidate measurements via an acquisition objective (e.g., expected information gain about a decision boundary), and iterating until the remaining uncertainty is below a chosen tolerance.
